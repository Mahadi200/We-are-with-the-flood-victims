Convernor

Assalamualaikum I am a general student of Ashraful Islam Tushar Northern University. I was active from the beginning of the anti-discrimination student movement and brought freedom to the country by shedding my blood from the royal path. Floods caused by India became the biggest problem during this post-independence period. Because of which millions of people in our southern region lost their homes and many lost their lives and are living inhumane lives. Seeing this situation, I could not sit any longer. I had a meeting with my class friends and we divided into 4 teams along with helping ourselves and we collected funds day and night from people around us (Airport, Uttara, Khilkhet, Banani) and even trains and from common people. In that our total amount of money stands at 1 target 4 thousand taka. We could have given this money to various NGOs or foundations if we wanted, but then we talked to one of our elder brothers whose house is in Noakhali. They have been stuck in the water for 3 days without any relief. So we decided that we will go to Sar Zamin and help everyone in that village. For this purpose we have contacted the local representatives there and with their overall support we are taking dry food, primary medicines, napkins, purified water for 200+ families tonight and 1000+ packets of khichuri are being arranged in the shelter centers due to food shortage in those villages. able to do In ShaAllah tomorrow is going to be a difficult day for us, everyone will pray for us.

Co-convenor

Assalamualaikum I am a general student of Mahin Northern University. I was wondering what to do about the current flood situation as I could not sit at home as a frontline fighter of the anti-discrimination student movement. Just then my friend Ashraful requested us all to stand by the flood victims with our help. Since then I tried my best to fund collection and last 29.08.24 with my friend Ashraful I and some of our friends left for Noakhali and throughout the day we distributed relief gifts to the flood victims there. Initially we help 200+ families with dry food, medicines and essential items. And managed to organize a meal for 500+ people sheltered in the shelter. We will continue this activity. Wishing everyone help and prayers.
